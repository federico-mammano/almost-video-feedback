The tedious part

Describing UI bugs to an AI shouldn't take longer than the fix.

The old way

  • Type a wall of text into the prompt
  • Stop, take a screenshot, find it in Downloads
  • Drag it in, then explain which part you mean
  • Repeat. For. Every. Single. Issue.

With Almost Video Feedback

  • Hit record.
  • Say "this button's misaligned and the total's wrong" while you point at them.
  • Hit stop. The bundle is on your clipboard.
  • Paste into your agent. Hit enter. Done.

How it works

Four steps. Most of them are just talking.

Record

Click the toolbar icon or hit a hotkey. A tidy bar shows you're live, with a timer and shot count.

Talk & point

Describe what's wrong while you click, select text, scroll, or circle the mouse. It transcribes you and auto-captures the right frames.

Stop

Click the icon again. It bundles transcript + screenshots and copies a ready-to-go prompt to your clipboard.

Paste & run

Paste into Claude Code or any agent, hit enter, and wait for the fix. It reads your words and the screenshots in order.

Under the hood

Strategic about what it captures.

It's aggressive about catching the right moment and ruthless about not flooding your agent with near-identical frames.

🎙️

Real-time transcription

Your voice becomes text live in the overlay — no API key, runs in your browser.

📸

Smart screenshots

Captures on clicks, text selection, scroll-stops, and mouse-circling — then dedupes near-identical frames with a perceptual hash.

🧩

Correlated bundle

Every screenshot is tied to what you said at that moment, the page URL, and the element you touched.

📋

One-paste handoff

On stop, a prompt + the file path are already on your clipboard. Paste, enter, go.

🎞️

Recordings library

Review past sessions, prune screenshots you don't want, and re-open any of them.

📤

Share as a zip

Bundle a session into a .zip and send it over email, Slack, or WhatsApp.

Get started

Up and running in about a minute.

install.md
  1. Download almost-video-feedback.zip from the latest release and unzip it somewhere permanent.
  2. Open chrome://extensions and turn on Developer mode (top-right).
  3. Click Load unpacked and pick the unzipped folder.
  4. Pin it, then allow the microphone the first time you record on a site.
github.com/JustinWi/almost-video-feedback/releases/latest
update.md
  1. From a release: download the newest .zip, unzip over the old folder (replace), then…
  2. From a clone: run git pull in your local copy.
  3. Go to chrome://extensions and click the ↻ reload icon on the card.
  4. If prompted, re-accept permissions. That's it — your recordings library is preserved.
git pull && # then reload at chrome://extensions
Your data

It stays on your machine.

No accounts, no servers, no telemetry — because there are no servers. Screenshots and transcripts are written to your own Downloads folder and the extension's local storage, and nothing else.

● Local by default

Recordings live in your Downloads and in the extension's IndexedDB. The bundle never leaves your device unless you share the zip.

● Honest about voice

Transcription uses Chrome's built-in Web Speech API, so audio is processed by the browser's speech service (Google) — the same as voice typing. No other data leaves.

● Auditable

MIT-licensed, no build step, no dependencies. Read every line, run the tests, and CI checks every contribution.
